The History of Emojis

Emojis began in Japan in the late 1990s when Shigetaka Kurita created the first set of 176 emojis for a mobile internet platform. These small digital icons were designed to help people express emotions, ideas, and information quickly in text messages. Over time, emojis became popular worldwide as smartphones and social media grew. In 2010, emojis were added to the Unicode Standard, allowing them to be used across different devices and platforms. Today, emojis are an important part of online communication, helping people add emotion, humor, and personality to their messages.
